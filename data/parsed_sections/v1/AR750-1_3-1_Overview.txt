
3–1.  Overview  
a.  Army maintenance will preserve the required performance capabilities of Army materiel or return 
those assets to their baseline performance capabilities.  
b.  Army maintenance will follow the principle that t he useful service life of Army equipment is achieved 
when the item is operated within its intended purpose and is maintained in accordance with its designed 
or engineered specifications. When an equipment item reaches its useful service life the Army will use 
acquisition, recapitalization, rebuild, or overhaul statement of work to replace or extend the equipment 
service life.  
c.  The Army will use four core maintenance processes to manage equipment during its useful service 
life: performance observation, eq uipment services, fault repair, and single standard repair. These pro-
cesses are defined in DA Pam 750  –  1.
